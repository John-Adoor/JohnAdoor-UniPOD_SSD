Design Thinking: Laptop Stand

Applying Human-Centered Design to Create an Ergonomic Solution

Design Thinking Process Summary

  • Problem Focus: Creating an ergonomic laptop stand for remote workers and students
  • Design Thinking Stages: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test
  • Key Insights: Users need adjustable, portable solutions that reduce physical strain
  • Solution Concepts: 12 innovative laptop stand ideas generated
  • Target Users: Remote professionals, students, digital nomads
 

Introduction

This design thinking process applies the human-centered approach to creating an improved laptop stand. With the rise of remote work and digital learning, people are spending more hours on their laptops, often in non-ergonomic setups that lead to physical discomfort and reduced productivity.

Following the 5-stage design process, we'll explore user needs, define the core problem, generate creative solutions, and develop prototypes for an innovative laptop stand that addresses real user pain points.

Process Documentation

Stage 1: Empathize - Understanding User Needs

Through interviews with 5 remote workers and 3 students, I gathered insights about their laptop usage patterns, pain points, and desired improvements for laptop stands.

Empathy Map
SAYS
  • "My neck hurts after hours of looking down at my laptop"
  • "I wish my stand was easier to carry in my backpack"
  • "The angle options on my current stand are too limited"
  • "It takes up too much space on my small desk"
THINKS
  • Worries about long-term posture damage
  • Frustrated by single-purpose furniture
  • Wants a professional setup even in small spaces
  • Values aesthetics but prioritizes functionality
DOES
  • Stacks books under laptop as makeshift stand
  • Frequently adjusts seating position
  • Carries laptop between home, office, and coffee shops
  • Uses external keyboard and mouse with laptop
FEELS
  • Frustrated by physical discomfort
  • Anxious about productivity loss due to discomfort
  • Overwhelmed by cluttered workspace
  • Disappointed by current market options

Stage 2: Define - Problem Statement

Based on the empathy research, I identified the core problem and formulated a clear problem statement using the provided formula.

Problem Statement

University staff and students need a way to easily adjust their laptop to their desired heights and angles in various environments because current solutions are in the market, have limited adjustability, or take up too much space, leading to physical discomfort and reduced productivity.

Stage 3: Ideate - 12 Creative Concepts

Following the ideation rules (quantity over quality, defer judgment, encourage wild ideas, build on others), I generated 12 diverse concepts for laptop stands.

Selection Criteria
Feasibility

Can we build it with available resources and manufacturing methods?

Desirability

Do users want and value this solution? Does it address their core needs?

Viability

Is it practical, cost-effective, and sustainable to produce?

12 Concept Ideas
1. Modular Stack System

Interlocking modules that can be stacked to create different heights and configurations

Features: Multiple angle options, collapsible, space-efficient
2. Collapsible Tripod

Three-legged stand that folds flat for portability with adjustable leg lengths

Features: Ultra-portable, stable base, quick setup
3. Book-Style Foldout

Hardcover book design that opens to reveal multiple stand positions

Features: Discreet design, multi-position, lightweight
4. Magnetic Panel System

Magnetic panels that attach in different configurations to create custom angles

Features: Infinite configurations, modern aesthetic, easy storage
5. Adjustable Arm Mount

Clamp-based system that attaches to desk edge with fully articulated arm

Features: Maximum adjustability, space-saving, professional look
6. Inflatable Cushion Stand

Air-filled ergonomic cushion with multiple surface angles when inflated to different levels

Features: Ultra-lightweight, customizable firmness, compact storage
7. Telescoping Rail System

Extendable rails with locking positions for precise height and angle control

Features: Precise adjustments, sturdy construction, modern industrial design
8. Origami-Fold Design

Laser-cut sheet metal or plastic that folds into multiple stand configurations

Features: Single-piece construction, artistic design, multiple positions
9. Suction Cup Wall Mount

Removable suction cup system that attaches laptop to walls or glass surfaces

Features: Space-maximizing, versatile mounting, quick release
10. Rotating Turntable Stand

Motorized rotating platform with adjustable tilt for presentation and collaboration

Features: 360Â° rotation, remote control, presentation-focused
11. Bamboo Spring-Loaded

Sustainable bamboo construction with spring-loaded height adjustment mechanism

Features: Eco-friendly materials, one-hand operation, natural aesthetic
12. Desk-Integrated Pop-Up

Stand that retracts flush with desk surface and pops up when needed

Features: Seamless integration, space-saving, premium look

Stage 4: Prototype - Selected Concepts

Based on the ideation phase, I selected the most promising concepts and created annotated sketches to communicate the key features and functionality.

Top Selected Concept: Modular Stack Stand

This concept scored highest across all selection criteria, offering excellent adjustability, portability, and space efficiency.

Annotated sketch of the modular stack stand showing key features and materials
Key Features
  • Interlocking Modules: Each module connects securely with locking mechanism
  • Multiple Angles: Each module offers 3-4 different angle options
  • Collapsible Design: Modules nest inside each other for storage and transport
  • Non-Slip Surface: Rubberized coating on contact points
  • Cable Management: Integrated channels for cable organization

Reflection

The design thinking process provided a structured yet flexible approach to developing a user-centered laptop stand. The empathy phase was particularly enlightening, revealing that users' frustrations extended beyond simple height adjustment to include portability, space efficiency, and quick adaptability.

Generating 12 distinct concepts challenged me to think beyond conventional laptop stand designs. The "quantity over quality" rule during ideation proved valuable, as some of the most innovative ideas (like the origami-fold and magnetic panel systems) emerged later in the process after exhausting more obvious solutions.

The modular stack system emerged as the strongest concept because it uniquely addressed all key user needs identified in the empathy phase. Its modular nature allows for custom configurations while maintaining excellent portability and storage efficiency.

This process has demonstrated how human-centered design can transform everyday objects into solutions that genuinely improve people's daily experiences and wellbeing. The iterative nature of design thinking means these concepts will continue to evolve based on user feedback, potentially leading to even more refined solutions.

Moving forward, I would proceed to the Test phase with physical prototypes to validate the concepts with users, particularly focusing on the modular stack system's usability and the collapsible tripod's stability as backup option.
